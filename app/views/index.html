Shutter pages

A shutter page is a page that is shown when a service has been switched off on purpose. These are different to page not found and technical difficulties pages.

Wherever possible, a shutter page should include:

  • the day, date and time the service is going to be back
  • other ways for someone to do what they need to do, if there are any

Have a general page that does not include the day, date and time so a service can be shuttered quickly, before the content can be updated. But update the content as fast as possible.

The page should:

  • have “Service unavailable” as the page title and H1
  • include the service title to reassure people they are in the right service
  • not have alpha or beta banners
  • not have breadcrumbs
  • be clear and concise
  • not use vague terms like maintenance or jargon like private beta
  • not use red text to warn people

Service closed 1

This is for a service, like tax credit renewals, that is only open for part of the year.

  • Example 1: a service that has a contact page on GOV UK
  • Example 2: a service that has a contact number, but no contact page
  • Example 3: a service that has no offline support

Service closed 2

This is for a service that has been permanently closed. You should try and provide information about what has replaced it or what people need to do if they have outstanding questions.

  • Example 1: a service that has a contact page on GOV UK
  • Example 2: a service that has a contact number, but no contact page
  • Example 3: a service that has no offline support

Not available yet

This is for a service that has not opened yet. This could be for tax credit renewals, which opens in April, or for a service that has been published but is not open until a specific day. These pages may be the same as the day, date and time pages.

  • Example 1: a service that has a contact page on GOV UK (contact details have been removed to prevent phone calls)
  • Example 2: a service that has a contact number, but no contact page
  • Example 3: a service that has no offline support
